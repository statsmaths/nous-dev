THE NOUS CONCEPT

NOUS arose from a simple observation: Louisiana and the Gulf South’s French and Creole cultures are reenergizing the region and are positioned to create new cultural and business opportunities.

NOUS supports and amplifies the full potential of these opportunities. A nonprofit organization based in New Orleans, Louisiana, NOUS is raising funds to build a globally renowned cultural institute dedicated to French and Creole. This dynamic space will feature three components: a thought-leading idea lab that will host workshops, conferences, and networking events; a vibrant café that will serve as a social space; and a concept store dedicated to the French-speaking world. The institute will also sponsor local and global exchange by providing scholarships and grants across disciplines.

OUR VISION FOR NOUS

NOUS brings us together—French for us, NOUS promotes Louisiana and the Gulf South’s French and Creole cultures and reinforces our role as an integral part of the French-speaking world.

Today presents a unique opportunity because Louisiana recently became the first U.S. state to join the International Organization of the Francophonie, through an initiative that NOUS’s founders launched and spearheaded. The Francophonie brings together 88 regions and countries with over 1 billion people. NOUS is channeling this momentum into a vibrant, people-oriented space where local and global exchange across the French-speaking world becomes a reality.

NOUS brings new possibilities—Pronounced new, NOUS is reimagining how a cultural institute can be a creative, social, and economic pillar.

Ideas thrive when we create together, and a diversity of languages unlock new ways of thinking. Louisiana and the Gulf South retains its unique legacy of the largest concentration of French and Creole speaking communities in the United States. These languages have the full potential to create new opportunities. We do not have to look far: knowledge of French in Canada’s two most bilingual provinces, Quebec and New Brunswick, boosts annual trade by $9 billion because their bilingual services make them a preferred partner for French-speaking businesses from around the world. Inspired by these possibilities, NOUS will provide an experience where everyone can learn, socialize, and create in a multilingual space.

THE NOUS EXPERIENCE

Dynamic—The programs and events will be as interactive as they are innovative, embodying the ingenuity around us.

Collaborative—NOUS brings together the young and young at heart who are striving not only to preserve the region’s culture but also to find new ways to promote its potential.

Tangible—Our guiding strategic vision supports an economic, cultural, and linguistic ecosystem around French and Creole.

Accessible—All of our programs and events showcase the creativity and potential of our local communities to everyone.

Dynamic—collaborative, tangible, and accessible: The NOUS idea is about getting the entire community involved.

With your help, NOUS wants to build a dynamic community and a world-renowned cultural institution based in the Gulf South that can foster partnerships and develop programs that amplifies the cultural and economic potential that only our region has in the United States.

MEET NOUS’S FOUNDERS

Scott Tilton was born in New Orleans where he grew up learning French and Creole. He lived the past several years in Paris where he worked as a consultant at Ernst & Young (EY) France for the Government & Public Sector practice. He has worked extensively on performance improvement and project management, notably for the European Union, the French government, and the United Nations. He holds a dual B.A. in Political & Social Thought and Foreign Affairs from the University of Virginia as well as an M.S. in International Relations from Sciences Po Paris. A Louisianan through and through, Scott is excited to come home to the city he loves where you can always find him out listening to live music or drinking coffee in the French Quarter.

Rudy Bazenet is a native of France and was based in Paris prior to moving to New Orleans. He has seven years of experience as a diplomat with the French Ministry of Foreign Affairs. He has worked extensively with the United States on project management, international cooperation, and cultural outreach. As part of his career, he has lived and worked in several countries, including the United States, Slovenia, Canada, and Iceland. Rudy holds a dual B.A. in International Economics and English/American Studies as well as an M.S. in International Affairs from Paris Dauphine University. He is bilingual in English and French, proficient in Spanish and Italian, and an avid learner of Creole. Always passionate about learning more about his adopted home, you can find Rudy exploring Frenchmen Street, strolling along the river, or reading on a sun-soaked café patio.
